Adware

It is a type of software that displays advertisements on a computer or mobile device.

Antivirus

Anti-Virus solutions integrate the latest generation of virus detection technology to protect users from viruses, spyware, trojans, and worms that can infect equipment through email or internet browsing.

Blacklist

Synonymous with Block List, the term "Blacklist" is widely falling into disuse following the growth of the Black Lives Matter movement.

Blocklist

A list or databases of users, systems, companies, etc. that are specifically prohibited from accessing a system (e.g. an email server would reject inbound email from any domain that was on the organisation's Block List).

Botnet

A collection of innocent computers which have been compromised by malicious code in order to run a remote control agent granting an attacker the ability to remotely take advantage of the system's resources in order to perform illicit or criminal actions. These actions include DoS flooding attacks, hosting false Web services, spoofing DNS, transmitting SPAM, eavesdropping on network communications, recording VOIP communications and attempting to crack encryption or password hashes

Bug

An error or mistake in software coding or hardware design or construction. A bug represents a flaw or vulnerability in a system discoverable by attackers and used as point of compromise. Attacks often use fuzzing technique (i.e. randomize testing tools) to locate previously unknown bugs in order to craft new exploits.

BYOD

A company’s security policy dictating whether or not workers can bring in their own devices into the work environment, whether or not such devices can be connected to the company network and to what extent that connection allows interaction with company resources

CAPTCHA

are designed to prevent automated software (bots) from performing actions that degrade the quality of service of a given system, such as spamming forms or brute force attacks.

Cryptography

The application of mathematical processes on data-at-rest and data-in-transit to provide the security benefits of confidentiality, authentication, integrity and non-repudiation.

CyberBullying

involves aggression conducted via electronic means, often due to the perceived anonymity of the internet.

Cybersecurity

The efforts to design, implement, and maintain security for an organization's network, which is connected to the Internet. It is a combination of logical/technical-, physical- and personnel-focused countermeasures, safeguards and security controls.

Dark Web

is a segment of the Deep Web, which includes all parts of the internet not indexed by standard search engines like Google.

Data Breach

The occurrence of disclosure of confidential information, access to confidential information, destruction of data assets or abusive use of a private IT environment.

Data Integrity

A security benefit that verifies data is unmodified and therefore original, complete and intact. Integrity is verified through the use of cryptographic hashing.

DoS (Denial of Service)

An attack that attempts to block access to and use of a resource. It is a violation of availability. DOS (or DoS) attacks include flooding attacks, connection exhaustion and resource demand.

DDoS (Distributed Denial of Service)

An attack which attempts to block access to and use of a resource. It is a violation of availability. DDOS (or DDoS) is a variation of the DoS attack (see DOS) and can include flooding attacks, connection exhaustion, and resource demand.

Encryption

is a process used to secure data by converting it into a code to prevent unauthorized access.

Endpoint Protection

refers to a comprehensive approach to securing endpoint devices—such as computers, laptops, smartphones, and tablets—from cyber threats.

Fileless Malware

is a type of malicious software that operates in memory rather than relying on traditional files stored on a hard drive.

Firewall

A security tool, which may be a hardware or software solution that is used to filter network traffic. A firewall is based on an implicit deny stance where all traffic is blocked by default.

Hacker

A person who has knowledge and skill in analyzing program code or a computer system, modifying its functions or operations and altering its abilities and capabilities.

Hacktivism

Attackers who hack for a cause or belief rather than some form of personal gain. Hacktivism is often viewed by attackers as a form of protest or fighting for their perceived “right” or “justice.”

IDS (Intrusion Detection System)

A security tool that attempts to detect the presence of intruders or the occurrence of security violations in order to notify administrators, enable more detailed or focused logging or even trigger a response such as disconnecting a session or blocking an IP address.

IoT

refers to a network of interconnected devices embedded with sensors, software, and other technologies that enable them to collect and exchange data over the internet.

Malvertising

is a term that combines "malware" and "advertising," referring to malicious advertisements that are used to deliver harmful software to users' devices.

Parental Controls

A social engineering attack that attempts to collect information from victims. Phishing attacks can take place over e-mail, text messages, through social networks or via smart phone apps.

Ransomware

Scareware

A form of malware that holds a victim's data hostage on their computer typically through robust encryption. This is followed by a demand for payment in the form of Bitcoin (an untraceable digital currency) in order to release control of the captured data back to the user.

Sniffing

refers to the process of intercepting and analyzing network traffic to gather information, often without the knowledge or consent of the parties involved.

Social Engineering

An attack focusing on people rather than technology. This type of attack is psychological and aims to either gain access to information or to a logical or physical environment.

Spam

A form of unwanted or unsolicited messages or communications typically received via e-mail but also occurring through text messaging, social networks or VoIP. Most SPAM is advertising, but some may include malicious code, malicious hyperlinks or malicious attachments.

Spear Phishing

A form of social engineering attack that is targeted to victims who have an existing digital relationship with an online entity such as a bank or retail website.

Spoofing

The act of falsifying the identity of the source of a communication or interaction. It is possible to spoof IP address, MAC address and email address.

Spyware

A form of malware that monitors user activities and reports them to an external their party. Spyware can be legitimate in that it is operated by an advertising and marketing agency for the purpose of gathering customer demographics.

Vishing

A form of phishing attack which takes place over VoIP. In this attack, the attacker uses VoIP systems to be able to call any phone number with no toll-charge expense.

VPN (Virtual Private Network)

A communication link between systems or networks that is typically encrypted in order to provide a secured, private, isolate pathway of communications.

Vulnerability

Any weakness in an asset or security protection which would allow for a threat to cause harm. It may be a flaw in coding, a mistake in configuration, a limitation of scope or capability, an error in architecture, design, or logic or a clever abuse of valid systems and their functions.

White Hat & Black Hat

White hat – Black Hat are terms to describe the ‘good guys’ and ‘bad guys’ in the world of cybercrime. Blackhats are hackers with criminal intentions. White-hats are hackers who use their skills and talents for good and work to keep data safe from other hackers by finding system vulnerabilities that can be fixed.

Cyberattack

Any attempt to violate the security perimeter of a logical environment.

Cyberespionage

The unethical act of violating the privacy and security of an organization in order to leak data or disclose internal/private/confidential information.

Zombie

A term related to the malicious concept of a botnet. The term zombie can be used to refer to the system that is host to the malware agent of the botnet or to the malware agent itself.

IP Security

IP Security (IPsec) is a suite of protocols designed to secure Internet Protocol (IP) communications by authenticating and encrypting each IP packet in a communication session. It provides data integrity, confidentiality, and authentication, making it essential for virtual private networks (VPNs) and secure data exchanges over the internet.

CERT

The Computer Emergency Response Team (CERT) is an organization that provides expertise and support in responding to cybersecurity incidents. CERT teams analyze threats, develop response strategies, and assist organizations in improving their security posture through education and awareness programs.

ANSSI

The Agence nationale de la sécurité des systèmes d'information (ANSSI) is the French governmental agency responsible for enhancing the security of information systems. ANSSI provides guidance, conducts security audits, and develops national cybersecurity policies to protect critical infrastructure and information assets.

Protocol

A protocol is a set of formal rules and conventions that dictate how data is transmitted and received over a network. Protocols define the syntax, semantics, and synchronization of communication between network devices, ensuring reliable and efficient data exchange (e.g., HTTP, TCP/IP, FTP).

Proxy

A proxy server is an intermediary server that acts as a gateway between a user’s device and the internet. It prevents direct access to the web by routing requests through itself, offering benefits like anonymity, content filtering, and improved security by hiding the user's IP address from the target server.

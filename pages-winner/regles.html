Cicada-3032 Rules

Welcome to Cicada-3032

Cicada-3032 is a board game focused on cybersecurity. The goal is to be the first to reach the center of the board. You can use cards to help yourself or to hinder your opponents.

Game Contents:

  • 1 game board
  • 4 game pieces
  • 3 dice (1d10, 1d6, 1d3)
  • 64 "draw" cards (30 bonus cards, 30 malus cards, 4 protection cards)
  • 32 "trap" cards (various effects)
  • 32 "quiz" cards

Setting Up the Game:

  • Place the board in the center of the table with the card decks next to it.
  • Each player chooses a game piece and places it on the starting space.
  • Deal 5 cards to each player and add the protection cards to the draw deck.
  • Shuffle each card deck.
  • Start playing.

Turn Structure:

  • Draw Phase: The player draws a card.
  • Pre-card Phase: The player may play a bonus card or a malus card.
  • Movement Phase: The player rolls a die and moves their piece forward the number of spaces shown.
  • Space Effect Phase: The player resolves the effect of the space they landed on.
  • Post-card Phase: The player may play another bonus card or malus card.

Types of Spaces:

  • Draw Space: Draw a card from the draw deck.
  • Trap Space: Draw a card from the trap deck.

Space Rules:

DMZ (Demilitarized Zone):

  • The DMZ is a safe zone where players cannot be targeted by card effects.
  • To advance, they must correctly answer a quiz question.
  • When a player enters the DMZ, they draw a quiz card and must answer it.
  • If they answer correctly, they can move forward on their next turn. If they answer incorrectly, they must answer another quiz question on their next turn before they can advance.
  • DMZ Rules:
    • If a player must move back and reaches the DMZ, they must answer another quiz question to advance.
    • A player in the DMZ cannot be targeted by others.

Cards:

Types of Cards:

  • Bonus Card: Provides a positive effect to the targeted player.
  • Malus Card: Applies a negative effect to the targeted opponent.
  • Protection Card: Grants protection against a specific type of card.

Card Rules:

  • Malus: A player can only be affected by one card of a given type at a time (for example, a player cannot be hit by two "virus" cards simultaneously).
  • Protection: When a player uses a protection card, they become immune to the associated card type until they reach the next SAS. Once they reach that point, the protection card is discarded. A player can have only one active protection card at a time.
